What happens to your pet if something happens to you?

A pet trust is a simple way to make sure your pet is cared for — with both a caregiver and dedicated funding in place.

You do not have to solve every detail today. This page walks through the key questions most pet owners ask first.

Can someone really take care of your pet without a plan?

Pets cannot inherit money directly. Many owners assume a friend or relative will step in, but long-term care usually needs both instructions and funding. A pet trust helps connect care and funding in one structured plan.

So how does a pet trust actually work?

Step 1
You choose a caregiver

You identify who will provide day-to-day care and set clear instructions.

Step 2
You set aside funds

Money is allocated to support care over your pet’s remaining life.

Step 3
A trustee manages the funds

The trustee follows your instructions and oversees distributions.

Step 4
Care is supported over time

The caregiver receives support as care expenses arise.

Who is responsible for what?

Each role has a clear responsibility:

Caregiver

The person who directly looks after your pet day to day.

Trustee

The person or trust company that manages the trust funds according to your instructions.

Remainder Beneficiary

The person or organization that receives any remaining trust funds after your pet passes away.

Care and money are intentionally separated to help protect your pet.

Does the caregiver receive all the money upfront?

Usually, no. In most structures, the trustee or trust company manages distributions over time according to your instructions. Funds can support food, veterinary care, medication, grooming, boarding, emergency care, and end-of-life expenses.

How much control do you actually have?

You can include practical, detailed instructions for how your pet should be cared for.

  • Feeding schedule and nutrition preferences
  • Veterinarian preferences and treatment guidance
  • Medication details and medical alerts
  • Exercise routines and behavioral needs
  • Sleeping arrangements and preferred living environment
  • End-of-life wishes and oversight frequency

This isn’t just funding — it’s a complete care plan.

When does a pet trust actually take effect?

A pet trust is generally structured to become active when the owner dies or can no longer care for the pet, depending on how the plan is drafted and executed.

Why not just include this in a will?

WillCan name a caretaker and leave money, often without detailed ongoing fund-management instructions.
Pet TrustProvides added structure for how money is managed and used over time for your pet’s care.

Is this legally binding right away?

PawLegacy helps you prepare a structured planning draft. Final legal enforceability depends on attorney review, execution, notarization where required, and funding.

FAQ

Can my pet legally receive money? ▾
Not directly. A trust structure is used to manage funds for your pet’s benefit through designated people or entities.
Who controls the funds? ▾
The trustee controls and distributes funds according to the trust instructions.
How much money should I set aside? ▾
It depends on your pet’s age, expected care horizon, and medical needs. PawLegacy provides a planning estimate to help you start.
What if my caregiver can’t continue? ▾
You can name a backup caregiver and successor trustee so care continues with fewer disruptions.
Can I include detailed care instructions? ▾
Yes. You can include practical instructions about routines, health, environment, and oversight.
Is this different from a will? ▾
Yes. A will can name caregivers and assets, while a pet trust provides clearer ongoing rules for how care funds are managed.

Frequently asked questions

Is a pet trust actually legally binding? ▾
Pet trusts are recognized in many U.S. jurisdictions, but enforceability depends on your state law and proper execution. PawLegacy generates a planning draft designed to support attorney review, notarization, and funding steps.
Do I need an attorney to use PawLegacy? ▾
You can draft your plan in PawLegacy, but we strongly recommend licensed attorney review before execution. This product is a legal-tech planning workflow, not a law firm or legal advice service.
What happens to the trust funds? ▾
Funding and disbursement are planning guidance only. Final account structure and distributions are established during attorney-reviewed execution with appropriate financial institutions.
Can I update my trust after creating it? ▾
Yes. You can regenerate and update your planning draft as your pet’s needs change. Any final legal updates should still be reviewed in your execution documents.
