Frequently asked questions

Is a pet trust actually legally binding? ▾
Yes — pet trusts are legally recognized in all 50 U.S. states. They are the only legally enforceable way to leave funds specifically for your pet's care. PawLegacy generates documents that meet your state's specific requirements.
Do I need an attorney to use PawLegacy? ▾
No. Our platform automates the legal drafting process. We recommend having a personal attorney review the document if you have a complex estate, but it's not required for most pet owners.
What happens to the trust funds? ▾
Funds are held by a professional trust company partner. When the trust is activated, the designated caregiver receives regular disbursements according to the care plan you defined.
Can I update my trust after creating it? ▾
Yes — Premium Plus subscribers get automatic legal updates and can modify their trust document anytime through the platform dashboard.
